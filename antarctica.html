A walk through Antarctica

Before I ever visited Antarctica, my perception of it was the same as everyone else’s - that it is just endless miles of plain, unadulterated snow. But it is so much more. I went to Antarctica when I was 14 and it is - by FAR - my favorite place on Earth.

Background info:

My family chose Quark Expeditions to go to Antarctica with. This is one of the few expeditions that goes to Antarctica from Chile instead of Argentina - and we wanted this because the Chilean Antarctic base (where we would enter the continent) has an airplane runway, so there is a choice to fly there, instead of sailing across the Drake passage.

We went in December 2016 (summer in Antarctica) and the cruise lasted 6 days.

Places we visited (sailing through the Lemaire Channel):

  • King George Island
  • Portal Point
  • Orne Harbour
  • Port Lockroy
  • Deception Island

    • Rules we had to follow:

  • Not feeding the animals
  • Covering up holes we made in the snow (because tiny penguins can fall in them and would have a hard time finding their way out)
  • Not littering anywhere
  • Not going within 6 feet of the penguins, as it tends to scare them
  • Don’t stand in a doorway. The ocean waves in Antarctica are rough at times and this can lead to doors slamming on you.

    • My reflections on it and why it was my favorite trip ever:

    Whenever I go on holiday, I love trying new things and learning as much as I can from seeing and doing new things. What made Antarctica special is that the place itself has such a strong appeal, even before you get into sightseeing over there. My favorite memories in Antarctica can’t be attributed to specific locations or activities. But experiences! Most of them unbelievable, other-worldly experiences that were unlike anything I could have imagined before.

    There is so much that I saw that I wish I could see again for a first time! Penguins. Icebergs. Glaciers. (Learning the difference). Icebergs melting into the sea. The midnight sun. Miles and miles of pristine nature. Did I mention penguins?

    OH and so many things I wish I could do again for the first time! Riding a zodiac. Writing a postcard to home from Port Lockroy. Snow trekking. Whale watching. The list goes on!

    These experiences were a snippet of what made it such an incredble trip, and I realized Antarctica is the closest thing we have left in the world to untouched human nature. It struck me at some point that I was a whole CONTINENT away from any civilization. All in all, it showed me a side of the world I never had fathomed could exist. It is beautifully tucked away, and, in my opinion, a living metaphor of a “hidden gem”.

    Antarctica is filled with hidden sights and things to do that are guaranteed to bring a smile to your face - in the moment, and also when you reflect on them years later (and while writing a blog post about it!). I truly understood how the little things - the things that may not initially constitute to much - can have so much significance and bring you so much joy.

    And finally, the people that I met on the cruise are ones that I keep in touch with to this day. The connection that was formed by exploring the most wonderful part of the world with them has prevailed over time, even though I spent just 6 quick days with them.

    So from the polar water to watching penguins run around, from wearing parkas to sporting rubber boots and from seeing the midnight sun to snow trekking for the first time. I love love love Antarctica - all the way to the South Pole and back.
